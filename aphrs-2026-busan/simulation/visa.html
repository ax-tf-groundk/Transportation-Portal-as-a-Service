Visa Information

Entry requirements for the Republic of Korea. All visitors must hold a valid passport.

Nationals of visa-free countries can enter Korea for tourism or temporary visits without a visa. Visa-free entry does not automatically mean K-ETA exemption — most visa-free nationals still need K-ETA. Always confirm your own requirement on the official Korea Visa Portal ↗, including whether a notarized invitation letter is required.

Related links: Ministry of Foreign Affairs ↗ Immigration Bureau ↗ Korea Visa Portal ↗ K-ETA ↗

🛫 Korea Electronic Travel Authorization (K-ETA)

K-ETA is mandatory for nationals of visa-free countries entering Korea without a visa. Apply at least 72 hours before boarding via the website (www.k-eta.go.kr) or the K-ETA mobile app. The application fee is non-refundable; once issued, K-ETA is valid until its expiration date.

ⓘ The temporary K-ETA exemption (Visit Korea Year) has been extended through 31 December 2026 (KST). The countries/regions below are currently exempt.
