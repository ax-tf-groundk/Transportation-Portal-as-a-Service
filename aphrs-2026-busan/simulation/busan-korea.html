Explore Busan, Korea

A vibrant port city of beaches, culture, and unforgettable food on Korea's southeast coast.

Busan, a vibrant port city in South Korea

Busan is a vibrant and bustling port city located on the southeastern tip of the Korean peninsula. It is the second-largest city in South Korea after Seoul, serving as a major economic, cultural, and transportation hub in the country.

The city experiences four distinct seasons, with late October being optimal for enjoying its attractions. Known for its beaches, cultural heritage, and lively atmosphere, Busan welcomes domestic and international visitors.

Why Busan: unique features and attractions
🏖️ Beaches and coastal hotspots
🌊
Haeundae & Gwangalli Beaches

Sandy coastlines and clear waters positioned near the conference venue. The Gwangandaegyo Bridge adds to the allure of the nighttime coastal views.

⛰️
Taejongdae

A scenic natural park that boasts breathtaking cliffs and picturesque trails.

🗼
Busan Tower (Yongdusan Park)

Panoramic views of Busan's skyline and the surrounding port area.

🏛️ Historical landmarks
🛕
Beomeosa Temple

Established in 678 AD, representing significant Buddhist architecture.

🌅
Haedong Yonggungsa

A temple set along the cliffside, offering beautiful sea views.

🕊️
UN Memorial Cemetery Park

From the era when the city served as a temporary capital during the Korean War — a space for contemplation on global friendship.

🛍️ Shopping and entertainment
🏬
Shinsegae Centum City

The world's largest department store, close to the conference venue.

🛒
Nampo-dong

Near Busan Station — street shopping and local goods.

🦑
Jagalchi Market

The largest seafood market in South Korea, where visitors can enjoy a vast array of fresh seafood, including fish, crabs, octopus, and more.

🎭 Cultural attractions
🎬
Busan International Film Festival (BIFF)

Held annually, bringing together a diverse selection of films from around the world, captivating filmmakers, actors, and film enthusiasts alike.

🎮
G-STAR

One of the world's top 3 gaming events, held annually at the BEXCO conference venue.

🎨
Gamcheon Culture Village

Celebrated for its vibrant art installations and charming narrow alleyways.

About Korea

Nestled in Northeast Asia, the Korean peninsula is surrounded by three sides of water, with the beauty of four distinct seasons.

Over 70 years, South Korea achieved significant economic and technological growth. The nation ranks as the 11th largest economy globally (as of 2016) and excels in mobile phones, semiconductors, automobiles, chemicals, and steelmaking.

The Hallyu (Korean wave) phenomenon encompasses music, television shows, movies, gaming, and webtoons. South Korea has transformed from being the world's most impoverished nation to one of the top 10 exporters globally.

Tourist-Friendly Korea
🛡️

Safe Streets & Low Crime Rate

With low crime rates, the streets of Korea are quite safe. Tourist Police operate in major tourist areas to guide and assist visitors.

🌙

A Country that Never Sleeps

Convenience stores, restaurants, malls, and ATMs operate 24/7 year-round.

🪧

Foreigner-friendly Signs

Most public places have English signs; major attractions feature English, Japanese, and Chinese signage.

🏥

Excellent & Fast Medical Services

Advanced facilities with skilled professionals and excellent clinic-to-hospital referral systems.

📶

Wi-Fi Heaven

Subway stations, coffee shops, malls, and most tourist areas offer fast and free Wi-Fi.

💳

No Cash, No Worries!

Most places accept credit cards, even for small purchases.

🚇

Convenient Public Transportation

Extensive subway, nationwide rail, and express buses. A transport card gives free transfers between subways and city buses.
